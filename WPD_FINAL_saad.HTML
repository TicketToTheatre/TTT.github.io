1.The Kapil Sharma Show

The Kapil Sharma Show, also known as TKSS, is an Indian Hindi-language stand-up comedy and talk show broadcast by Sony Entertainment Television.
Comedian Kapil Sharma interacts with celebrity guests about their latest films while keeping the audience laughing with his wit, humour and assorted skits.
The Best Comic Show That Will Leave You Rib-Tickled.
The filming of the show took place at Film City situated in Goregaon East, Mumbai.
A total of 374 episodes have been aired on the show with 130 being in the first season, 177 being in the second and 67 being in the third.

2.Koffee With Karan

Koffee with Karan is an Indian talk show on Star World India hosted by film producer, actor, and director Karan Johar.
With his effortless wit and suave persona, Karan Johar has his celebrity guests spilling the tea on love, work and everything in-between.
The most talked-about chat show of the tinsel town, where host Karan Johar makes the celebrities bare their deepest and darkest secrets on the Koffee couch!
A total of 135 episodes of this famous chat show have been aired being divided into six seasons for the viewers to enjoy. According to the producers, the concept of the show revolves around "spilling the beans while having a hot cuppa." They have stated that Johar is "the brain behind the show" and that he "sits on everything".

3.Son Of Abish

Son Of Abish is a variety comedy talk show with host Abish Mathew. It is a Disney Plus Hotstar Original Series.
This power packed show has a hilarious monthly news take down & features special celebrity guests who talk & perform for the audience. Comedian Abish Mathew invites two celebrity guests to engage in some fun and games, and gives a lowdown on current affairs in his hilarious, inimitable style.
Son Of Abish has hosted some popular faces including Vidya Balan, Irrfan Khan, Mithila Palkar, Abhay Deol,Bhuvan Bam, Kenny Sebastian to name a few.If you are looking to unwind after a long day or just some moments of genuine laughter, this comedy talk show is it for you.

6.Satyamev Jayate

Host Aamir Khan interacts with the general public of India and aims to enlighten citizens about the social issues present in the country. Satyamev Jayate is an Indian Hindi-language television talk show aired on various channels within Star Network along with Doordarshan's DD National. The first season of the show premiered on 6 May 2012 and marked the television debut of popular Bollywood actor and filmmaker Aamir Khan. The second season of the show was aired from 2 March 2014 and the third season started from 5 October 2014. The show also received positive feedback from various eminent personalities such as social activists, media houses, film and television personalities. Bal Thackeray, the founder and chief of the political party Shiv Sena praised the show and Khan for bringing out social issues in front of public.

7.Back Benchers

Back Benchers is an Indian celebrity quiz show hosted by film producer and director, Farah Khan. The show debuted on Flipkart Video in October 2019, featuring Anil Kapoor and Shilpa Shetty as the first guests.
First episode date: 19 October 2019
Number of episodes: 20
Language: Hindi
Number of seasons: 1
Original network: Flipkart Video
Genre: Game show.
The show consists of three segments, after each round, the scores are revealed and the guest with the most points wins.

1.Oral test:
In this round, questions are asked to the guests to test their general knowledge.
2.Written test:
The guests are asked to write down their answers on the blackboard.
3.Speed test
The segment includes a rapid-fire Q and A with Farah asking guests a similar set of questions.
4.Step of the day:
Farah challenges her guests to replicate a pre-decided step of the day which is then thrown open to TikTok users.

8.Aap Ki Adalat

Aap Ki Adalat is an Indian television show that is hosted by Padma Bhushan journalist Rajat Sharma. The show began in 1993, was broadcast on the television channel Zee TV until 2004, moved on from Zee News in 2004, and now airs on India TV. It is the longest running show in India's television history.The host invites various personalities and interacts with them discussing their work, personal life and achievements, and also engages in a debate on current political issues. A regular weekend feature on India TV, the show has consistently ruled television ratings for more than twenty years. The interview of Narendra Modi just before the 2014 general election broke all rating records, attracting 74 per cent of Hindi news television viewers in India.
